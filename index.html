Nelson Samayoa

Professional software developer since 2010, graduate of Oregon State University. My original real passion in life is language learning. I don't mean linguistics specifically, although that does of course interest me as well. I mean literally learning languages for fun. To most people it's a chore to be avoided but for me it's how I relax. The feeling of being able to decipher something that only a few hours ago was completely meaningless is a feeling I'm probably addicted to. Fortunately for me, this natural drive helps me a lot as a software developer because ideally every day is a day in which I come to understand something that I did not understand when I woke up that morning.

I have to make an effort keep myself from having too many hobbies because there are so many things that interest me. This page describes some of the main topics that make my life interesting (in addition than family and friends of course). This site is just a little introduction to myself and made in super simple HTML+CSS+JavaScript, which is nice to do sometimes.

I started learning C# in 2009 and in 2010 I started my internship at Intel in Oregon. It was at Intel that I fell for C# and the .Net way of doing things. When I started my internship most of my experience had been in C++, which is common for Computer Science students at Oregon's universities. C# and .Net just felt more enterprise, in a good way. It just felt like the right language and tooling to use when you had to make software that absolutely worked and was maintainable. This sentiment has gotten strong over the years and as I complete a decade as a C# developer I'm grateful to me from the past for having chosen such a great language as my primary programming language. I feel like I've grown alongside .Net and now .Net Core. I was there when WinForms was common, and WPF was still new. When WFC was still king among enterprise and MVC was a new way of architecting a web app. I'll also still be there as .Net Core grows and matures to be an even better framework than it already is. Enough emotion, let's talk tech...

Angular is my framework of choice for writing web clients in an enterprise (and personal) environment.

Up until 2019, Ionic using Angular was my go-to framework for mobile development. The reasons for that may be obvious since I not needing to learn a new language and framework is a huge advantage.
